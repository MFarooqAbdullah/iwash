Fama Platform Privacy Overview

We are strongly committed to letting you know how we will collect and use your personal information. The policies below are applicable to Fama Technologies, Inc.’s network of platforms and services, including web.fama.io, and all apps published by Fama (“Platforms”).

Please be advised that the practices described in this Privacy Policy apply only to information gathered online at our Platforms. It does not apply to information that you may submit to us offline, or to Sites, as defined below, or by Sites maintained by other companies or organizations to which we may link or who may link to us.

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or don’t see your concerns addressed here, you should contact us by email at privacy@fama.io.

Fama Technologies, Inc. complies with the EU-U.S. Privacy Shield Framework as set forth by the U.S. Department of Commerce regarding the collection, use, and retention of personal information transferred from the European Union to the United States. Fama Technologies, Inc has certified to the Department of Commerce that it adheres to the Privacy Shield Principles. If there is any conflict between the terms in this privacy policy and the Privacy Shield Principles, the Privacy Shield Principles shall govern. To learn more about the Privacy Shield program, and to view our certification, please visit https://www.privacyshield.gov/.

The two sections below (Fama for Businesses and Fama for Individuals) offer an overview of our platform privacy policies. While many websites simply include a privacy policy governing access to their marketing websites, we thought to include an additional layer of transparency about the information we collect when businesses enlist the use of our Platforms.

Fama For Businesses: FCRA and EEOC Compliance

FCRA. Fama considers itself a consumer reporting agency when businesses pay Fama to use its services for decisions related to hiring or medical staffing, as such, Fama complies with the Fair Credit Reporting Act in the United States. Fama offers the disclosure and authorization forms, relevant state-by-state disclosures, pre-adverse and adverse notification letters, and a variety of other, up-to-date FCRA materials and best practices to ensure that your business stays compliant with the evolving legal landscape. In addition, Fama offers individuals the opportunity to opt-out of having their online identity collected by Fama.

Privacy Shield. Fama commits to cooperate with EU data protection authorities (DPAs) and comply with the advice given by such authorities with regard to human resources data transferred from the EU and Switzerland in the context of the employment relationship.

Additionally, Fama has responsibility for the processing of personal information it receives under the Privacy Shield and subsequently transfers to a third party acting as an agent on its behalf. Fama shall remain liable under the Principles if its agent processes such personal information in a manner inconsistent with the Principles, unless the organization proves that it is not responsible for the event giving rise to the damage.
