Online screening for:Healthcare

Keep your healthcare system free of toxic behavior.

While healthcare professionals have been rated the most trusted professions in the US, the reality of intimidation and abuse in the industry are also undermining the public trust. As the number of HIPAA violations and reports of abusive behavior increase year afteryear, we’re seeing that countless instances of toxic behavior are being found on social media and the public web.

Take control of your biggest risks

Extensive discrimination and bullying

While most physicians and caregivers are doing the best job they can in high-pressure situations, some are actively intimidating staff and causing dangerous medical errors in the process. Others are harassing or discriminating against patients directly, putting both patient outcomes and patient safety at risk.

“Wrong medication? Tell me one more time and I’ll punch u in the face”

A loss of public trust

Some surveys suggest that doctors and nurses are the most trusted professions. In reality, trust has been declining for decades. Today, only 34% of Americans have confidence in medical leaders due to a range of issues, from drug diversion and medication errors to the ongoing and widespread sexual abuse of vulnerable patients.

“New batch of inpatients started today, cant wait to spend a few overnights with the hot blonde”

Mounting HIPAA violations

Many healthcare professionals are now sharing their latest patient interactions on social media. Whether it’s a physician complaining about a specific patient in their latest blog or an emergency unit employee sharing photos and mocking someone in their care, more and more providers are putting patient privacy at risk in a whole new way.

“Pic from last nite, patient was waaaasted lol”
